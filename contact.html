Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Iris dataset?

The Iris dataset is a classic dataset introduced by British statistician and biologist Ronald Fisher in 1936. It contains measurements of 150 iris flowers from three different species: Setosa, Versicolor, and Virginica.

How accurate is your model?

Our logistic regression model achieves approximately 95% accuracy on test data. While this is good, it's important to note that predictions are based on statistical patterns, and occasional misclassifications can occur.

Can I use your model for my own iris classification?

Yes! Our web app is freely available for educational purposes. However, for professional or research applications, we recommend training a model on your specific dataset for the most accurate results.

How do I properly measure iris flowers?

Measure the sepal length from the base of the flower to the tip of the sepal. For width, measure at the widest point. Do the same for petals, making sure measurements are in centimeters. For consistent results, use a caliper or ruler with millimeter markings.
