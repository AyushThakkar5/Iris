About the Iris Dataset

The Iris flower dataset is one of the most famous datasets in the field of machine learning and statistics. Introduced by British statistician and biologist Ronald Fisher in 1936, it has become a foundational example for pattern recognition and classification problems.

The dataset consists of 150 samples from three species of Iris flowers:

  • Iris Setosa - A distinctive species that is easily separable from the other two
  • Iris Versicolor - Shares some characteristics with both other species
  • Iris Virginica - The largest of the three species

For each sample, four features were measured:

  • Sepal length (in centimeters)
  • Sepal width (in centimeters)
  • Petal length (in centimeters)
  • Petal width (in centimeters)

These measurements provide the basis for our machine learning model to distinguish between the three species. Our implementation uses Logistic Regression, a statistical model that analyzes the relationship between features to predict categorical outcomes.
