Iris Species Prediction

Enter flower measurements to classify the Iris species

How It Works

Our classifier uses a Logistic Regression model trained on the classic Iris dataset. This statistical model has learned to recognize patterns in the four key measurements of Iris flowers to distinguish between three species:

Iris Setosa

Known for its small petals and wide sepals. Native to eastern Siberia.

Iris Versicolor

Medium-sized flower with distinctive blue to purple colors. Found in North America.

Iris Virginica

Largest of the three, with broader petals. Native to eastern North America.

To use the classifier, simply enter the measurements in centimeters for your Iris flower in the form below. The model will analyze these measurements and predict which species your flower belongs to.

Measurement Guide

Measure the sepal length from the base to the tip. Measure the width at the widest point. Apply the same procedure for the petals.

Dataset Insights

Key Observations

Iris Setosa is linearly separable from the other two species, but Versicolor and Virginica have some overlap in their features.

Feature Importance

Petal dimensions tend to be more discriminative than sepal dimensions for species classification.

Model Accuracy

Our logistic regression model achieves approximately 95% accuracy on test data.
